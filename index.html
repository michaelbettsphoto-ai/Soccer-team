Practice Plans (First 6)

Each practice follows a consistent format: warm-up, two skill activities, small-sided game, scrimmage/cooldown, and coaching points.

Practice 1

Ball Familiarity & Fun

Objective: Build comfort on the ball and introduce team expectations.

  • Warm-up: Toe taps + dribble freeze game
  • Activity 1: Red light/green light dribbling
  • Activity 2: Dribble through gates
  • Small-sided game: 3v3 free play
Practice 2

Dribbling Under Control

Objective: Keep close control while changing speed and direction.

  • Warm-up: Touches with both feet
  • Activity 1: Cone slalom races
  • Activity 2: Sharks and minnows
  • Small-sided game: Dribble end-zone game
Practice 3

Passing & Receiving Basics

Objective: Introduce accurate short passing and first touch.

  • Warm-up: Partner one-touch passing
  • Activity 1: Passing through gates
  • Activity 2: 3v1 rondo
  • Small-sided game: 4-goal passing game
Practice 4

Shooting Technique

Objective: Improve confidence and mechanics in finishing.

  • Warm-up: Passing to shot progression
  • Activity 1: Finishing from dribble lane
  • Activity 2: 2v1 to goal
  • Small-sided game: Bonus points for shot on target
Practice 5

Defending Fundamentals

Objective: Teach pressure, patience, and angle of approach.

  • Warm-up: Shadow jockey footwork
  • Activity 1: 1v1 channel defending
  • Activity 2: Transition win-the-ball game
  • Small-sided game: Defend then counter
Practice 6

Game Application Day

Objective: Combine dribbling, passing, shooting, and defending in live play.

  • Warm-up: Team passing circuit
  • Activity 1: Conditioned 3v3
  • Activity 2: 4v4 with touch limits
  • Small-sided game: Final scrimmage festival

Parent Recap

Our first six practices focus on fundamentals and confidence. Ask your player to show one move and one pass they learned after each session.
